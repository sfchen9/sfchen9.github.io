Welcome

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate at Department of the Geophysical Sciences, the University of Chicago, advised by Prof. Sunyoung Park, focusing co-seismic displacements produced by deep earthquakes.

My research interest is mainly about using different observations and methods to better understand the structure and internal processes of the Earth, especially the deep Earth. Another goal of my research is to examine how the deep Earth can interact with and influence shallow processes. I am also interested in the application of new techniques in seismology, e.g., 3D printing.

Prior to this, I obtained a B.S. degree in Geophysics from SUSTC (Southern University of Science and Technology of China). My undergraduate research, which was advised by Prof. Chunquan Yu, focused on upper mantle earthquakes in Tibet.
